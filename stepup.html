StepUp--The fashion app

Introduction

"Dress for the job you want." But how?
People especially female graduates often do not know what the transition is going to be like when they first start their career after college.
And learning how to dress for your work is very important when you first start your first dream job or internship, and we have hard time finding a good fashion app on the market to give them good and useful fashion advice.
StepUp is here to help. The purpose of the app is to curate the settings and the feed according to users' taste, and we will show them what they like, and what are on sale.
We work with instagram and many social platforms to gather information in the fashion world, and generate a feed for the user so they can pick and choose, get inspired, and purchase the items.

Team members: Boyu Zhang, Christine Chang.
My role: Web designer, UX designer.

Problems

Price is important concerns for freshly graduate people.
Uncertainty about the dress code of the industry causes stress and affects performance.
There is no easy way to compare and shop all the brands at once.
It is hard to go through tedious and confusing on-boarding process.

Solutions

Easy on-boarding process.
Shop the best deals all year round.
Discover items curated to users' industry and style.
Gather new fashion ideas from various social media platforms, inspire people to dress like influencers and professionals.
Save, like, and share items from all the brands at once, no need to jump from different platforms anymore.

User story

Name: Karina P.
Age: 23
We have talked to her and done some research. She just graduated from the design department at University of California Irvine, and is about to start her first job at a design firm in Los Angeles.
She would like some outfits and items that more professional and sleek for her new job, but does not know where to start. She needs some fashion suggestions on how to dress trendy and professional at the same time. She also needs recommendations curated based on her profession, and in the meantime not spend too much money.

Colors and logos

The colors we chose are peachy, pinky tones and earthy tones.
We wanted to incorporate circles, organic shapes with the word UP together and the finalized version reflects our value the most.
Based on that concept, we also wanted to make the app and the landing page look very sleek, simple yet very friendly.
