Pop up clinic and App

Introduction

In Seattle, about 12,112 people are currently experiencing homelessness across the county,
Of those, more than half were living unsheltered, including in tents, in vehicles, and on the street.
According to our research, 73% of the respondents reported at least one unmet health need,
and they are often neglected.

As a healthcare service, Thrive tries to tackle the problem, we provide services include:
Healthcare packages, Pop-up Clinic, mobile app and more.
Our mission statement: At Thrive, our mission is to make healthcare convenient and accessable
to people experiencing homelessness.
Our goal is to connect our clients to resources and empower them to be in charge of their own wellness.

Team members: Boyu Zhang, Christine Chang.
My role: Visual designer, UX designer.

Problems

1. Being stigmatized for being homeless.
2. Disrespectful treatment Feeling ignored by health care providers.
3. Lack of health insures Inability to purchase or acquire medications.
4. Lack of healthcare knowledge.
5. Long wait time Finding resources for food, clothing, and shelter is the first priority.
6. Mental illness and substance abuse Denial of health issues.
7. Fear of losing their children if found to be homeless.

Solutions

1. A medium to healthcare and other resources that promote wellness.
2. A safe environment to comfortably share information Approachable services.
3. Interaction with respect & dignity Human connection & social support.
4. Confidentiality Healthcare as priority Trackable health reports for future refences.
5. Collaboration with multiple social services & health specialties.
6. Free & safe transportation Familiar user patterns based on current mental models.

User story

Name: Cynthia L.
Age: 28
Cynthia is a single mother with a 3-year-old girl who was a stay home mom.
Due to series of events, she escaped from her abusive husband but seriously injured. There is nowhere for her to go as she has no money.
She has been sleeping in her car with her daughter.
It is getting cold out there. Her daughter started having fever, but they do not know any resources, they wish somebody can come and help.

Name: Ruth
Age: 54
Occupation: None
Ruth has been married to her husband for 20 years, and has been suffering from sexual abuse.
Recently, their neighbor called the police after a loud event.
The court filed restraining order against Ruth's husband. She cannot return home because he owns the property. Due to years of abuse she constantly suffers from chronic pain, trauma disorder, and depression.
Ruth feels hopeless and insecure, and she really needs mental support and physical medical care to treat her chronicle diseases.

Branding

Forms follows function, the colors we chose are warm, cheerful and friendly,
Oranges and yellows are often used as healthcare and hospitality.
The two fonts we chose are Lato and Montseratt, both are sans serif, and have low x-height.
We also worked with organic shapes, curvy lines, and rounded corners.

Sponsorship and Partnership

To make sure we give our customers the best services, we are working with Solid Ground.
Solid Ground is a non-profit organization located in Seattle that provides different services for homeless people.
Their goal is to build a community and to eliminate homelessness in Seattle one day.
The services include: providing housing resources, legal advice, transportation and other benefits.

One of their most important services is the Downtown Seattle Circulator Bus.
It provides rides for people living on low or no incomes and those who access health and human services in the downtown area.
There are 9 stops on the map and our Thrive app will be map based, giving users live updates 24/7,
and our Pop-up Clinic will be at one of the stops.

Thrive app--Wireframe

The primary view is a map and it will give live update and show where our pop-up clinic is.
Users can also check out other nearby foodbanks, hospitals and other resources in Downtown Seattle.
Chosen task flow: the user logs in and check out location/service information on the map.

On boarding process

In-person sign up at Thrive pop-up clinic:
1. Nurses and volunteers will be available at the pop-up clinic.
2. Volunteers will take over new patients after the nurses have acquired basic medical data.
3. The volunteers will help new patients to download the Thrive app, and assist them to create an account to link to their medical record

In-app:
1. Two factor authentication: Full name & birth date.
2. Extra security: secret question.

Thrive Care package

As the first customer touch point, our care package is very important.
It is a 2 gallon ziplot bag that contains life essentials and product.
On the bag there is bar code to encourage people to scan and download the Thrive app.
These packages are handed out to the people living outdoors, and also handed out at our pop-up clinics.

Inside the packages include: Socks, toothbrush & toothpaste, tampons, first aid kit, condoms, soap, etc..
