Don't You Worry Honey--the album

This album is by Sir Sly.
Don't get fooled by the beats, this album stabbed my heart.
Nothing is like goodbyes and deaths to make make you face to who you are:
for better or worse.
In loss there is gain, the problem is that usually we have to lose someone else
to gain from ourselves.
We are all codependent, no matter we realize that or not.
The painful truth is, if you ask most people,
they fears losing a loved one more than losing themselves.
As individuals, we often tie our humanity to our relationships,
which is where this album comes in.

The tracks like “Oh Mama”, “&Run”, “Altar” and “Change”
have the singer's singing to loss as a hurtful but inevitable teacher.
The beats of “High” and “Tripping” feel like the heartbeats from a mechanic heart.

The hardest part about healing is that you have to feel,
as human we tend to avoid things and sweep it under the carpet.
But past few years I am trying to take my heart out and make it constantly feel,
and dissecting everything.
There’s no filter, I absorb everything: bad, or good,
in order to learn and grow up.
I don't think I'm okay, I'm trying to be though.
