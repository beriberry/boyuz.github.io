I would encourage you to set really high goals. Set goals that, when you set them, you think they're impossible. But then every day you can work towards them, and anything is possible, so keep working hard and follow your dreams.

Ledecky first became interested in swimming when she was six years old. She demonstrated prodigious skills during her high school and joined the Nation's Capital Swim Club as a trainee.

In 2012 at the 2012 London Olympics, Ledecky for the first time won the gold medal for 800-meter freestyle, much to the surprise of many.

Record breaking, swimmer of the year.

Being trained by Yuri Suguiyama, Ledekcy's training consist of the combination of low intensity, aerobic, and high intensity, which makes her one of the strongest female swimmers.

“Do what you feel is best for yourself.”

As a Female World Swimmer of the Year, she broke world record five times. She was also named Associated Press Female Athlete, United States Olympic Committee Female Athlete of the Year, and Sportswoman of the Year by the Women's Sports Foundation.
